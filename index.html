About the Event

We are excited to introduce Boston College's first full-scale hackathon! Hack the Heights will feature corporate sponsors, tech talks, and prizes for beginners and seasoned hackers alike. Come to our kickoff and registration event Friday, April 21st at 6pm! Hacking will begin Saturday, April 22 at 11am and conclude Sunday, April 23 at 12pm.

Interested in learning more? See the FAQ below or reach out to bccssociety@gmail.com with any questions.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an invention marathon. Programmers, designers, builders and more come together to learn, build, and share their creations over the course of a few days. Hackathons are not limited to computer science majors â€” anyone who has an interest in technology and is eager to learn can participate in a hackathon. Not to be confused with illegal and unauthorized programming, "hacking" in this context means quickly and intelligently creating a real application that others can use.

I'm new to CS. Can I still come?

Yes! This event is open to students of all levels and backgrounds. Students new to programming are encouraged to come, hone their skills, and learn something new.

I've never seen a computer in my life. What can I do at a hackathon?

While most participants will have some level of programming ability, people of all backgrounds can contribute. This includes designers, entrepreneurs, and beginners looking to learn.

Am I allowed to work on a pre-existing project?

While you are allowed to work on an existing project, you are encouraged to begin something new the day of the event. Rapid innovation is in the spirit of a hackathon, and only new hacks will be considrered for prizes.

Who decides prize winners?

Three judges from BC and our sponsors will determine prize winners based on the criteria of creativity, complexity, and utility.

Do I need to be a BC student to attend?

Yes, only BC students are allowed at this year's hackathon. Check back next year if you are a non-BC student interested in attending!
